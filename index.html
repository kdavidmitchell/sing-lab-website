Social Intelligence and Narrative in Games

Bridging computer science, social dynamics, and the performing arts at UC Davis.

Our Mission

Founded at the intersection of artificial intelligence and interactive media, the SING Lab is dedicated to pushing the boundaries of how machines understand and generate human-like social interactions. We believe that the next leap in computing will not just be about processing data, but about understanding nuance, emotion, and story.


What We Do

Our work is deeply interdisciplinary. We draw upon computer science, cognitive psychology, and the performing arts to build robust computational models. Whether we are designing algorithmic storytellers that can dynamically adapt a narrative's pacing, or programming digital actors that understand the subtleties of stagecraft, our goal is to create systems that are expressively rich and highly responsive to human interaction.

Core Research Areas

Modeling Social Dynamics

Exploring how artificial agents can perceive, reason about, and participate in complex social interactions within digital environments.

Algorithmic Story Generation

Focusing on the computational authoring of complex narratives, creating systems that autonomously generate compelling dramatic arcs, dynamic plotlines, and deeply expressive story worlds.

Theatrical and Artistic Execution

Bridging computer science and the performing arts by developing systems that model stagecraft and seek to enable human-like artistic performances in virtual spaces.
